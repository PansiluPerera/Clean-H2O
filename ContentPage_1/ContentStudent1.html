ACCESS TO BASIC SANITATION NEEDS

"4 billion people lack access to basic sanitation services."
- The United Nations

Daily, millions of children in rural African countries struggle to get basic sanitation and clean water while these are regarded as fundamental rights in most other countries. It is a condition of disease and ignorance because people have to travel long distances to fetch water from contaminated sources. Lack of infrastructures and poor economic status are some of the unnecessary causes of young people's death due to diseases like cholera and diarrhea. There are promises in international alliances and community-oriented initiatives although these are some of the challenges encountered. It is possible to ensure that every child receives the minimum package of a healthy and dignified childhood, starting with clean drinking water and sanitation.

Image Source : Hygiene (UN official site)

In many settlements across the globe, access to proper sanitation is still a challenge and millions of people in urban slums are denied proper sanitation every day. Lack of proper toilets and sanitation facilities result to unhygienic conditions that are very dangerous to human health. Some of the diseases prevalent are hepatitis, typhoid, and respiratory diseases because of contaminated water and poor hygienic conditions. The female sex is often exposed to increased risks and loses her integrity in order to use unsafe public facilities or practice OD. While the local communities and groups have endeavored to change the state of affairs, the global intervention and investment in infrastructure are required to ensure the equal availability of acceptable sanitation for all the people living in urban areas.

Image Source : 559 community toilets in slums to be revamped

In most of the developing countries, inadequate sewage systems compound the sanitation challenges, posing severe health risks to the people. Raw sewage floods the drinking water supply; people get diseases like cholera and dysentery due to contaminated water. Garbage that is not properly disposed of also affects the environment; the cities and the countryside alike. Inadequate sewage systems are usually as a result of inadequate funding and political commitment. Nevertheless, addressing these issues through proper waste disposal and management and through funding for proper sewage systems can significantly enhance the health conditions of the public and the environment. It is therefore important that the sewage treatment is done in the right manner for the better living of every individual.
